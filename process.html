Case Study - the redesign of Northeast Minneapolis Art Association’s webpage

The Problem

During our research and interviews we discovered that artists need resources that NEMAA has to offer but don’t fully understand NEMAA’s purpose and goal.

The Solution

We believe redesigning NEMAA’s webpage for our community, artists, and stakeholders will achieve growth in NEMAA memberships and an overall increase in traffic to the Northeast Minneapolis arts scene.

Previous Next

We took a pretty traditional approach and kicked off the project with our user research. We worked through user research which lead us to the definition phase, and finished with creating the prototype. I planned and conducted the user interviews, created the user scenario and storyboarded the user path. User Research

Our user research commenced the project. I planned and conducted the user interviews, created the user scenario and storyboarded the user path. We interviewed artist, community members and a stakeholder to discover what route to take to encompass all aspects of the non-profit's growth.

Definition

During our definition phase, I took the lead on the competitor analysis and heuristics evaluation which aided me in discovering our key insights, problem statement and value proposition. These findings were key to measuring our progress and successfulness in our process.

Prototyping

I was heavily involved throughout all of the prototyping process. I focused on A/B testing plans, the clickable aspects of XD, and the homepage. My main contributions in our final stages were iterating the changes from our mid to high-fidelity wireframes.
